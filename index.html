Master Thesis
Master Thesis
My thesis suggests a new eye-tracking based adaptive method to simulate water using SPH. Using eye-tracking to identify where a user is looking it is possible to identify the area which has caught the focus of the user and this thesis explores the idea of high resolution simulations at the exact view point of the user.

My suggested method using eye-tracking is also compared to an already established surface based adaptive method through performance to show the potential benefits of limiting the high-resolution area of the simulation to where a user is looking.
